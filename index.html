I am a Junior at the University of Alberta in the BSc Honors Mathematics program. My undergraduate thesis supervisor was Prof. Bin Han in the field of Classical Fourier Analysis $\subset$ Harmonic Analysis.

My research interests lie in the fields of Harmonic Analysis and Partial Differential Equations (PDEs), with a specific focus on Fourier Analysis of PDEs and the theory and methods used to solve Nonlinear PDEs. Recently, the integration of Machine Learning techniques in solving PDEs has piqued my interest. I have begun delving into this topic and am currently in the process of learning and familiarizing myself with the various approaches and methodologies involved.

Currently, I am a research intern at the Max Planck Insitute for Dynamics of Complex Technical Systems in the Computational Methods in Systems and Control Theory Group. I am working under Mr. Yongho Kim and Prof. Jan Heiland. My research focuses on utilizing Convolutional Autoencoders and Deep Visualization Techniques to analyze the feature maps and weights of snapshots from spatially discretized incompressible Navier-Stokes equations within a cylinder.

Additionally, I am a research assistant working under Prof. Yaozhong Hu at the University of Alberta, in the fields of Random Matrix Theory and Fourier Analysis.

Summer '23

My current research at the MPI-magdeburg consists of extracting feature weights and maps from various models such as Convolutional Neural Networks, Convolutional Autoencoders (CAEs) and Individual CAEs (iCAEs)1 for the Navier-Stokes equations within a single and double cylinder and analyzing them.

For my research at the University of Alberta, Prof. Yaozhong Hu and I have been been thoroughly studying and analysing Clair et al.'s work2 Determinantal Point Processes for Image Processing. We aim to gain a better understanding of the paper and extract valuable insights from its content and findings.

Winter '23

During the Winter Semester of my third year, I did my undergraduate thesis under Prof. Bin Han. The title of my thesis is Linear Independence and Stability of Functions. I started off by studying prelimaninary material like Distribution Theory and Functional Analysis and then we proves some results like Linear independence of compactly supported distributions and relating the Kernel of the space of semi-discrete convolutions of a distribution to the Fourier transform of the distribution.

Writing this thesis has been enlightening, and I've gained a plethora of valuable knowledge. Despite the significant time commitment, I efficiently developed my paper writing skills through guidance and feedback from my supervisor.

We also had to do a write-up on a famous classical result in our field so I chose to do it on the Poisson Summation Formula. This has improved my ability to efficiently summarize complex concepts and communicate them effectively to others.

The full thesis, including the poster, presentation and the clasical result can be accessed here [Thesis], [Poster], [Presentation], [Write-up].
