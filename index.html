I am a Junior at the University of Alberta in the BSc Honors Mathematics program. My undergraduate thesis supervisor was Prof. Bin Han in the field of Classical Fourier Analysis $\subset$ Harmonic Analysis.

My research interests lie in the fields of Harmonic Analysis and Partial Differential Equations (PDEs), with a specific focus on Fourier Analysis of PDEs and the theory and methods used to solve Nonlinear PDEs. Recently, the integration of Machine Learning techniques in solving PDEs has piqued my interest. I have begun delving into this topic and am currently in the process of learning and familiarizing myself with the various approaches and methodologies involved.

Currently, I am a research intern at the Max Planck Insitute for Dynamics of Complex Technical Systems in the Computational Methods in Systems and Control Theory Group. I am working under Mr. Yongho Kim and Prof. Jan Heiland. My research focuses on utilizing Convolutional Autoencoders and Deep Visualization Techniques to analyze the feature maps and weights of snapshots from the spatially discretized incompressible Navier-Stokes equations within a cylinder.

Additionally, I am a research assistant working under Prof. Yaozhong Hu at the University of Alberta, in the fields of Random Matrix Theory and Fourier Analysis.
